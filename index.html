A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931 - 2015) was an Indian scientist, politician, and the 11th president of India: Early life: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam was the youngest of five children. He studied aeronautical engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology. Career: Kalam worked at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) for four decades. He was the project director for India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, the SLV-III, which launched the Rohini satellite into orbit in 1980. He was also responsible for the development of the AGNI and PRITHVI missiles. President: Kalam served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. He was known as the "Missile Man" and the "People's President" for his scientific achievements and popularity. Awards: Kalam received the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor. Legacy: Kalam is considered one of India's most inspirational leaders. He encouraged children to dream big and developed a special rapport with them. Autobiography: Kalam's autobiography is titled Wings of Fire. Death: Kalam died from cardiac arrest on July 27, 2015 in Shillong.
